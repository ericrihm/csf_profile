## Executive Summary

The Q1 2026 phishing simulation campaign tested all 300 Alma Security employees with two simulated phishing scenarios. The overall click rate dropped to 6.3%, continuing the downward trend from 18% in Q1 2025. The report-to-click ratio improved to 4.2:1, indicating a strong reporting culture. Engineering and Customer Success showed the strongest performance. Sales continues to have the highest click rate but improved significantly from prior quarters.

## Analysis

### Strengths

- Engineering and IT departments demonstrate strong phishing resilience, consistent with technical awareness
- The phishing@almasecurity.com reporting mechanism is well-adopted; employees actively report suspicious emails
- Supplemental training for clickers appears effective based on declining repeat clicker rates
- D&R team provides individual feedback to reporters, reinforcing the reporting behavior

### Areas for Improvement

- Sales & Marketing consistently has the highest click rate, though improving; department-specific awareness training recommended
- Leadership team had a small sample size (6 people) but 16.7% click rate warrants attention
- Attachment-based scenarios (Scenario 2) consistently produce higher click rates than link-based scenarios
- HR & Admin click rate is trending upward; needs investigation
- No vishing or smishing simulations conducted; employees are untested against voice and SMS vectors
