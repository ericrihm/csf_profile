# GV.RR-01: Leadership Accountability Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.RR-01 - Organizational leadership is responsible and accountable for cybersecurity risk and fosters a culture that is risk-aware, ethical, and continually improving

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review leadership accountability documentation**
   - Request Board charter and committee assignments for cybersecurity oversight
   - Verify CISO role and reporting line (CISO reports to CEO)
   - Confirm leadership roles and responsibilities for cybersecurity risk are documented
   - Check for documented authority and coordination mechanisms

2. **Examine security culture initiatives**
   - Review security awareness training program and participation rates
   - Verify leadership communications that promote security culture
   - Check for recognition programs or incentives related to security behavior

3. **Validate ethical security practices**
   - Review code of conduct or ethics policy for cybersecurity provisions
   - Verify vulnerability disclosure and reporting channels are established
   - Confirm leadership models expected security behaviors

4. **Interview CEO or Board member on cybersecurity governance**
   - Ask about Board's role in cybersecurity risk oversight
   - Verify quarterly reporting cadence is maintained
   - Confirm leadership investment in continual security improvement

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Board charter with cybersecurity oversight provisions
- [ ] CISO role description and reporting structure
- [ ] Security awareness training records and completion rates
- [ ] Leadership communications on security culture

## Notes

CISO reporting directly to CEO is a strong governance indicator. Assessment should verify this structure translates into genuine accountability rather than just organizational chart placement.
