# GV.SC-05: Supply Chain Requirements Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.SC-05 - Requirements to address cybersecurity risks in supply chains are established, prioritized, and integrated into contracts and other agreements with suppliers and other relevant third parties

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review cybersecurity requirements for suppliers**
   - Request standard security requirements included in supplier agreements
   - Verify requirements are tiered based on supplier criticality
   - Confirm requirements cover data protection, access controls, incident notification, and compliance

2. **Validate contractual security provisions**
   - Sample 3-5 supplier contracts for cybersecurity clauses
   - Verify right-to-audit provisions are included for critical suppliers
   - Check for SLA requirements related to security (vulnerability disclosure, incident notification timelines)
   - Confirm component inventory (SBOM) requirements for software suppliers

3. **Examine requirement prioritization**
   - Review how security requirements are prioritized based on supplier risk tier
   - Verify critical suppliers face more stringent requirements than low-risk suppliers
   - Check for information sharing and reporting protocols

4. **Interview legal/procurement on contract security clauses**
   - Ask how security requirements are incorporated into procurement process
   - Verify legal review of cybersecurity contract terms
   - Confirm process for updating requirements as threats and regulations evolve

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Standard supplier security requirements document
- [ ] Sample supplier contracts with security clauses
- [ ] Supplier tiering criteria and requirement mapping
- [ ] Right-to-audit and SLA provisions

## Notes

Assessment should verify requirements are not just boilerplate but are tailored to supplier criticality and enforced through ongoing monitoring.
