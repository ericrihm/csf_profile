# GV.RR-02: Roles and Responsibilities Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.RR-02 - Roles, responsibilities, and authorities related to cybersecurity risk management are established, communicated, understood, and enforced

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review documented roles and responsibilities**
   - Request cybersecurity RACI matrix or equivalent responsibility assignment
   - Verify roles documented in policy for key positions (CISO, Gerry, Nadia Khan, Chris Magann, Tigan Wang)
   - Confirm authorities are defined for risk acceptance, incident declaration, and escalation

2. **Validate job descriptions include security responsibilities**
   - Sample 3-5 job descriptions for security and non-security roles
   - Verify cybersecurity responsibilities are included in personnel descriptions
   - Check that performance goals include security-related metrics where applicable

3. **Examine responsibility communication**
   - Verify roles and responsibilities are communicated during onboarding
   - Check for annual review or re-communication of security responsibilities
   - Confirm responsibilities are articulated across operations, risk, and audit functions

4. **Interview team leads on role clarity**
   - Ask Nadia Khan (D&R Lead) and Chris Magann (VM Lead) about their documented authorities
   - Verify they understand their scope of responsibility and escalation paths
   - Confirm coordination mechanisms between security sub-teams

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Cybersecurity RACI matrix or responsibility assignment
- [ ] Job descriptions with security responsibilities
- [ ] Performance evaluation criteria including security metrics
- [ ] Onboarding materials with security role communication

## Notes

With a 15-person security team supporting a 300-person organization, clear role delineation is essential. Assessment should verify responsibilities are not just documented but actively understood by role holders.
