# GV.RM-07: Strategic Opportunities Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.RM-07 - Strategic opportunities (i.e., positive risks) are characterized and are included in organizational cybersecurity risk discussions and strategies

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review positive risk documentation**
   - Request evidence that strategic opportunities are documented alongside threats
   - Verify the risk register or equivalent includes positive risk entries
   - Check for stretch goals related to security capabilities

2. **Validate opportunity-risk integration**
   - Review whether security investments are framed as business enablers
   - Verify SOC 2 certification is documented as a positive risk (competitive advantage)
   - Check that security capabilities are positioned as sales enablement tools

3. **Examine innovation-security balance**
   - Review how security strategy balances risk mitigation with enabling innovation
   - Verify the approach avoids security-as-blocker mentality
   - Check for documented examples of security enabling new business capabilities

4. **Interview CISO on positive risk management**
   - Ask how strategic opportunities are identified and prioritized
   - Verify positive risks receive similar governance as threats
   - Confirm leadership reviews include both threats and opportunities

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Risk register entries for positive risks or opportunities
- [ ] Documentation of security as business enabler
- [ ] Strategic planning materials showing opportunity-risk balance
- [ ] SOC 2 business case documentation

## Notes

Alma's position as a security company creates natural positive risk opportunities. Their SOC 2 roadmap and trust-building initiatives serve dual purposes as risk mitigation and competitive differentiation.
