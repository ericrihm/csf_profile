# GV.RM-05: Risk Communication Lines Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.RM-05 - Lines of communication across the organization are established for cybersecurity risks, including risks from suppliers and other third parties

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review communication structure documentation**
   - Request org chart and communication flow for cybersecurity risk reporting
   - Verify reporting lines from security team to CISO to CEO to Board
   - Confirm cross-departmental communication channels are defined

2. **Validate upward reporting mechanisms**
   - Review quarterly Board reporting process and materials
   - Verify CISO has direct access to CEO for urgent risk communication
   - Check that cybersecurity risk escalation bypasses are defined for critical issues

3. **Examine cross-functional communication**
   - Verify engineering, product, legal, and HR have defined touchpoints with security
   - Check for regular cross-functional security meetings or briefings
   - Confirm third-party/supply chain risks are communicated to relevant business owners

4. **Interview non-security department heads on risk communication**
   - Ask 2-3 department leaders how they receive cybersecurity risk information
   - Verify awareness of how to escalate potential security risks
   - Confirm bidirectional communication (not just top-down from security)

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Organizational communication structure for cybersecurity risk
- [ ] Board reporting cadence and materials
- [ ] Cross-functional meeting schedules and minutes
- [ ] Risk escalation procedures

## Notes

CISO reports directly to CEO, which enables strong upward communication. Assessment should verify that lateral communication across departments is equally well-established.
