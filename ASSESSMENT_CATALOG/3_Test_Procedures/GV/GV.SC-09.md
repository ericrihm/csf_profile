# GV.SC-09: Supply Chain Security Practices Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.SC-09 - Supply chain security practices are integrated into cybersecurity and enterprise risk management programs, and their performance is monitored throughout the technology product and service life cycle

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review supply chain security integration in policies**
   - Request policies requiring provenance records for acquired technology
   - Verify policies address supply chain security across the product lifecycle
   - Confirm policies require approved personnel for maintenance of critical systems

2. **Validate provenance and integrity practices**
   - Check whether software provenance is tracked (SBOMs, signed artifacts)
   - Verify hardware procurement includes authenticity verification for critical components
   - Review upgrade and patch processes for unauthorized change detection

3. **Examine risk reporting on supply chain security**
   - Request evidence of supply chain security reporting to leadership
   - Verify acquired component security status is tracked and communicated
   - Check for regular communication between risk managers and operations on supply chain needs

4. **Interview Chris Magann (VM Lead) on supply chain vulnerability management**
   - Ask how third-party component vulnerabilities are tracked and remediated
   - Verify SBOM or equivalent tracking for critical software dependencies
   - Confirm patch management covers supply chain components

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Policies on technology provenance and supply chain security
- [ ] SBOM or software provenance records
- [ ] Supply chain security reporting to leadership
- [ ] Patch management records for third-party components

## Notes

As a SaaS company, Alma's software supply chain is particularly relevant. Assessment should verify that dependency management and vulnerability tracking extend to third-party libraries and services.
