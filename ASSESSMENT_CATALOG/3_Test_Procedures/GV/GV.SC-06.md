# GV.SC-06: Supplier Due Diligence Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.SC-06 - Planning and due diligence are performed to reduce risks before entering into formal supplier or other third-party relationships

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review due diligence process documentation**
   - Request documented due diligence procedures for new suppliers
   - Verify process includes cybersecurity assessment before contract execution
   - Confirm assessment criteria are consistent with procurement planning

2. **Validate supplier risk assessment execution**
   - Sample 3 recently onboarded suppliers and review pre-engagement assessments
   - Verify risk assessments evaluate cybersecurity capabilities and risk management practices
   - Check that assessment results inform contracting decisions

3. **Examine product and technology assessment**
   - Review process for assessing authenticity and security of acquired products
   - Verify software supply chain risks are evaluated (SBOMs, vulnerability history)
   - Check for technology suitability assessments before adoption

4. **Interview procurement team on due diligence practices**
   - Ask how due diligence integrates with vendor selection workflow
   - Verify security team involvement in supplier evaluation
   - Confirm due diligence findings can block or modify procurement decisions

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Due diligence procedure documentation
- [ ] Completed supplier risk assessments (recent samples)
- [ ] Product/technology security evaluation records
- [ ] Procurement workflow showing security checkpoint

## Notes

Due diligence should be proportional to supplier criticality. Assessment should verify the process is consistently applied and not bypassed under time pressure.
