# GV.OC-02: Stakeholder Needs Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** GV.OC-02 - Internal and external stakeholders are understood, and their needs and expectations regarding cybersecurity risk management are determined

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review stakeholder identification documentation**
   - Request stakeholder registry or equivalent mapping
   - Verify internal stakeholders identified (engineering, product, sales, legal, HR)
   - Verify external stakeholders identified (customers, regulators, partners, investors)
   - Confirm cybersecurity expectations documented per stakeholder group

2. **Validate customer-facing security expectations**
   - Review customer security questionnaire responses and patterns
   - Check enterprise customer contract security requirements
   - Verify SOC 2 roadmap addresses documented customer expectations

3. **Interview Gerry (IT/Security Manager) on stakeholder engagement**
   - Ask how internal departments communicate security needs
   - Verify process exists for collecting and prioritizing stakeholder requirements
   - Confirm security team tracks regulatory expectations (SOC 2, privacy)

4. **Review investor and Board expectations**
   - Examine Board Audit and Risk Committee charter for security expectations
   - Review investor due diligence security requirements from Series B

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Stakeholder registry or mapping document
- [ ] Customer security questionnaire response templates
- [ ] SOC 2 scoping documentation showing stakeholder requirements
- [ ] Board committee charter referencing cybersecurity expectations

## Notes

As a Series B company with enterprise customers, Alma faces multi-directional stakeholder expectations. Assessment should verify these are systematically captured rather than addressed ad hoc.
