# ID.RA-02: Cyber Threat Intelligence Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** ID.RA-02 - Cyber threat intelligence is received from information sharing forums and sources

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review Threat Intelligence Sources**
   - Obtain list of subscribed threat intelligence sources
   - Verify CISA advisory subscription is active
   - Confirm vendor security bulletin subscriptions
   - Check industry ISAC membership and participation

2. **Validate Intelligence Integration**
   - Review SentinelOne threat feed configuration
   - Verify detection rules are updated from intelligence sources
   - Check for automated indicator of compromise (IOC) ingestion
   - Confirm intelligence feeds are current and active

3. **Assess Intelligence Review Process**
   - Verify weekly threat briefing cadence and attendance
   - Review sample threat briefing content and distribution
   - Confirm advisories are distributed to relevant asset owners
   - Check for actionable intelligence follow-up tracking

4. **Review Intelligence Sharing Capabilities**
   - Assess STIX/TAXII implementation status
   - Check for structured intelligence sharing with partners
   - Verify information sharing agreements are in place
   - Review intelligence sharing with suppliers

5. **Evaluate Intelligence Correlation**
   - Check if threat intelligence is correlated with asset inventory
   - Verify vulnerability intelligence feeds into scanning priorities
   - Assess whether intelligence informs risk assessment updates
   - Review examples of intelligence-driven security actions

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Threat intelligence source subscription list
- [ ] SentinelOne threat feed configuration
- [ ] Sample weekly threat briefing
- [ ] Advisory distribution records
- [ ] ISAC membership documentation
- [ ] Intelligence-driven action examples

## Notes

Evaluate whether threat intelligence is actively consumed and operationalized versus passively received. The gap between receiving intelligence and acting on it determines effectiveness.
