# RS.CO-03: Incident Information Sharing Test Procedures

**CSF Subcategory:** RS.CO-03 - Information is shared with designated internal and external stakeholders

---

## Test Procedures

1. **Review information sharing policies and agreements**
   - Obtain incident information sharing procedures from the response plan
   - Verify information sharing agreements exist with key external partners and ISACs
   - Confirm procedures address data sanitization before sharing threat intelligence externally
   - Check that sharing protocols specify classification levels and handling requirements

2. **Examine internal communication and escalation practices**
   - Review Slack #security-alerts channel for incident status update cadence
   - Verify senior leadership receives regular status updates during major incidents
   - Confirm HR notification procedures exist for malicious insider activity
   - Check that cross-functional stakeholders (Legal, PR, Engineering) receive timely incident updates

3. **Validate external information sharing practices**
   - Review evidence of TTP sharing with external parties (ISACs, sector peers, threat intel platforms)
   - Verify sensitive data is removed before external sharing
   - Confirm contractual information sharing obligations with customers and partners are met
   - Check that supplier/vendor crisis communication protocols are documented and tested

4. **Test information sharing workflow execution**
   - Walk through a recent incident to verify information sharing steps were followed
   - Confirm internal stakeholders received updates per the defined cadence
   - Verify that sharing decisions were documented with justification
   - Check that information sharing did not compromise ongoing investigation or legal proceedings

---

## Evidence Requests

- [ ] Incident information sharing procedures
- [ ] Information sharing agreements with external parties
- [ ] Data sanitization procedures for external threat intelligence sharing
- [ ] Slack #security-alerts channel message samples during recent incidents
- [ ] Senior leadership incident briefing records
- [ ] HR notification records for insider-related incidents (if applicable)
- [ ] Supplier crisis communication protocols
- [ ] Evidence of TTP sharing with ISACs or sector peers

---

## Notes

This test procedure validates that the organization shares incident information appropriately with internal and external stakeholders. Effective information sharing requires balancing transparency with operational security. Key maturity indicators include predefined sharing agreements, automated internal notification via Slack, regular leadership briefings during major incidents, and voluntary external threat intelligence sharing with sensitive data sanitized. The existence of documented protocols for supplier crisis communication is particularly important given Alma Security's SaaS business model and customer obligations.
